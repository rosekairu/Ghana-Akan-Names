GHANAIAN AKAN NAMES

Names are cultural identifiers. The first gift you can give your child is a noble name, they wear it all their life and God willing live by the name given. Your name links you with your past, your ancestors and is a part of your spirituality.

Along the Gulf of Guinea lies the beautiful West African country of Ghana. Its culture and traditions are rich and vibrant. Akan Names are derived from the Akan people of Ghana who frequently name their children after the day of the week they were born and the order in which they were born.

A week after a child is born, the parents introduce it to the community, and community members celebrate with the family. Gifts that can be used in the home, such as firewood, are given to the woman, and money is given to the man.
